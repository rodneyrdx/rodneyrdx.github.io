How Plyground protects ownership, controls distribution, and restricts misuse
Core Principle
Asset creators keep ownership of the intellectual property they publish. Plyground receives only a limited, non-exclusive license to host metadata and previews, display assets on the platform, enable permitted use inside Plyground experiences, and operate the technical systems needed to run the ecosystem.

Protection Process
The agreement protects creators before upload, during distribution, and after violations
01
Publish Under Your Terms
The creator defines who can use the asset, whether it can be modified, and whether commercial use is allowed.
02
Select Distribution Model
Each asset can be designated Public, Secure, or Commercial depending on how much access and protection the creator wants.
03
Apply Technical Safeguards
Plyground may use controlled build pipelines, asset packaging protections, licensing enforcement, and cloud systems to reduce extraction risk.
04
Enforce Creator Rights
Creators can report violations, and Plyground may remove infringing content, restrict access, and suspend repeat offenders.

🤖
AI Data Protection
Creator-published assets are not used to train Plyground’s generative AI models unless the creator explicitly opts in through a separate program. The agreement also prohibits users from using platform assets to train generative AI models without permission.
🚫
Prohibited Uses
Users may not extract assets for reuse outside the creator’s license, scrape or download assets through automated systems, redistribute assets beyond permitted terms, or use them for AI training without authorization.
